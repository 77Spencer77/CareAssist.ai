The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World and the only one still standing today.
Ancient Egyptians invented one of the earliest writing systems - hieroglyphics - using pictures and symbols to represent words and sounds.
The Nile River, the longest river in Africa, was essential to Egyptian civilization providing water, transportation, and fertile soil for agriculture.
Tutankhamun's tomb, discovered by Howard Carter in 1922, contained over 5,000 artifacts and remains one of the most intact royal burials ever found.
Cleopatra VII was the last active pharaoh of Egypt and spoke multiple languages, though she was actually of Greek Macedonian descent.
Egyptian mummification was a complex 70-day process involving removal of organs, drying the body with natron salt, and wrapping in linen.
The ancient Library of Alexandria was one of the largest and most significant libraries of the ancient world, containing hundreds of thousands of scrolls.
Ancient Egyptians worshipped over 2,000 gods and goddesses, including Ra (sun god), Osiris (god of the afterlife), and Anubis (god of mummification).
